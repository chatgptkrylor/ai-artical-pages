Email Deliverability For Cake

A sharp, practical guide for marketers on balancing cadence, content quality, and technical hygiene to improve inbox placement.

Email Marketing·7 min read·Guide

A quick-start manual for take-home inbox success: cadence that respects readers, authentic signals, and clean DNS hygiene that keeps your emails out of the spam folder.

1) Cadence and Expectation Management

Your sending cadence should match subscriber engagement, not your marketing calendar. The first rule: send only when you have something worth saying. The second: set clear expectations in your welcome and preference center so subscribers know how often you’ll hear from them.

Over-emailing is a trust-breaker. Use engagement-based pacing: throttle the number of sends to inactive cohorts and widen windows for those who consistently engage. Tests show open and click-through rates rise when send frequency adjusts to actual engagement signals rather than a fixed schedule.

2) Authentication and DNS Hygiene

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC aren’t optional toys. They’re your passport to inboxes. The complexity lies in alignment: SPF should cover only your outbound servers; DKIM should sign every message; DMARC should reflect a policy your domain can actually enforce. Keep DNS TTLs sane, monitor reports, and retire stale subdomains that still advertise old sending identities.

3) Content Quality and Rendering

Content matters more than fancy tech in the inbox. A clean, scannable layout with semantic HTML reduces rendering issues and boosts engagement. A/B test subject lines with meaningful segment splits, and ensure your preheader text complements the subject for better first impressions.

4) Observability: Metrics That Matter

Open rate is flawed in the era of privacy. Replace it with to-the-point metrics: healthy click-to-open rate, conversion rate, and time-to-engagement. Use ESP webhook data to build a local dashboard that highlights deliverability incidents within 24 hours.

The Bottom Line

Deliverability is a system property: authentication, cadence, and content quality must align with user expectation. When you fix the basics, inbox placement follows.
