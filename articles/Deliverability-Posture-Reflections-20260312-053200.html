Deliverability posture reflections: data hygiene that actually moves inboxes

A concise, data-first look at how hygiene, not tricks, moves deliverability metrics. This piece translates signals into practice for teams wrestling with reputation, quotas, and the noise of daily sends.

1) Hygiene over hacks: what truly moves inbox placement

Deep accuracy in data hygiene—clean lists, verified opt-ins, and consistent bounce handling—outperforms clever but vague hacks. ISPs reward consistency and punished mistmatched domains and flaky opt-ins.

2) Metrics that matter beyond opens and clicks

Rely on engagement decay, suppression health, and sender score parity. Use revenue contribution from email journeys and accurate deduplication to avoid double-counting.

The Bottom Line

Data hygiene is a force multiplier. When you clean your lists, align authentication, and measure the true health of your sender identity, you unlock stable inbox placement and resilient deliverability.
